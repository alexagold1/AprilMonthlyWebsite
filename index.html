Chapter 1: Where Thunder Finds You

James loved two things: basketball… and storms. Whenever thunder rolled in, he’d race to the cracked court behind his neighborhood, where no one else dared play. There was something electric in the air — like the ball bounced better, like his jump was higher, like the wind wanted him to win. One night, the clouds turned violet.

Page 1

Rain poured down like silver needles, but James didn’t leave. He stayed, drenched, dribbling in the dark. Until the streetlights flickered… and then vanished. Then came a flash of lightning that split the sky. And when it faded… the world had changed. The court beneath his feet was no longer concrete — it was marble, veined with glowing lines. Floating stands rose around him, filled with ghostly figures in armor and robes. Banners snapped in the wind above them, each bearing strange symbols.

Page 2

In front of him stood a team — tall, masked players in silver capes. And across the court was an ancient scoreboard that read: House of Storm vs. The Challenger Score: 0 – 0 Quarter: Eternity The tallest figure, cloaked in midnight black, tossed him a glowing basketball and said: “You are summoned, Champion. Win, and claim your place in legend. Lose… and remain here forever.” James gripped the ball. It pulsed like a heartbeat in his hands. “What… what is this place?” The storm above rumbled in response. The whistle blew. And the Game of Kings began.
